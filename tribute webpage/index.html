Rosa Parks

The Quiet Woman Who Refused To Get Off The Bus


Timeline of Rosa Parks' life:

Early Years:

  • 1913 - February 4, Born in Tuskegee, Alabama, US

    • ~born Rosa Louise McCauley.

  • 1924 - Attended the Industrial School for Girls in Montgomery

    • ~She took acedemic and vocational courses.

  • Attended a laboratory school set up by the ASTCFN for secondary education.

    • ~She dropped out in order to care for her grandmother and lather her mother

  • 1932 - Ms. McCauley married Raymond Parks, a barber from Montgomery
  • 1933 - Mrs. Parks finished her high school studies

    • ~After being urged by her Husband, Raymond. At time when less than 7% of African Americans had a high school diploma.

  • 1943 - In December, Rosa Parks became active in the Civil Rights Movement, joining the Montgomery sector of the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People(NAACP)

    • ~She was elected as the secretary of the chapter until 1957

    "I was the only woman there, and they needed a secretary, and I was too timid to say no."
  • 1944 - Along with other civil rights activists, they organised the "Committee for Equal Justice for Mrs. Recy Taylor".

    • ~The Chicago Defender called it "the strongest campaign for equal juctice to be seen in a decade."

  • 1955 - After being encouraged and eventually sponsored by Clifford and Virginia Durr, Rosa Parks attended the Highlander Folk School, an education center for activism in worker's rights and racial equality.

    • ~She was mentored by the veteran organizer Septimer Clark


    Arrest & The Montgomery Bus Boycott:

  • December 1, 1955 Rosa Parks now 42 years old, commutes home from work at the Montgomery Fair department store by bus

    • ~Segregation was written into law; the front of the bus was reserved for white citizens and the back for black citizens - Negroes-in-back policy
    ~Though there were contradictory Montgomery laws: One said Segregation must be enforced, the other(largely ignored): no person could be asked to give up their seat if no other seats were available on the bus.
    ~At one point on the route back home, a man had no seat because all the seats in the "white" section were taken. The driver told the riders in the first row of the "colored" section to stand - effectively adding another row to the "white" section.
    ~Three obeyed, Parks did not. The bus driver stopped the bus.
    ~Two police officers approached the stopped bus, assessed the situation and came to a conclusion: Arrest Rosa Parks.

    “People always say that I didn’t give up my seat because I was tired,” wrote Parks in her autobiography, “but that isn’t true. I was not tired physically… No, the only tired I was, was tired of giving in.”
  • December 5, 1955 Rosa Parks was found guilty of violating segregation laws. She was given a suspended sentence and fined $10 plus $4 in court costs.

    • ~Black Montgomery citizens boycotted the buses on the day of Parks' trial.
    ~Due to an overwhelming response from the black community, E.D. Nixon and other activists formed the Montgomery Improvement Association(MIA) to manage the boycott.
    ~Reverend Martin Luther King Jr. as the newly elected president.

  • November 13, 1956 - The Supreme Court ruled bus segregation to be unconstitutional due to the national and international press focusing on all of the drama happening in Montgomery

    • ~The boycott ended on December 20, a day after the Court's written order arrived in Montgomery.
    ~In spite of Parks losing her job and having to deal with harassment all year, she came to be known as "The Mother of the Civil Rights Movement".


    Later Years

  • 1965 - Rosa Parks became an administrive aide in the Detroit office of Congressman john Conyers Jr. in 1965.

    • ~After facing continued harassment and threats, Parks and her family moved to Detroit
    ~She held this position until her retirement in 1988.

  • 1977-1979 - Rosa Parks lost her husband, brother & mother to cancer.
  • 1987 - She co-founded the Rosa and Raymond Parks Institute for Self-Development, to serve Detroit's youth.
  • 1999 - Rosa Parks was awarded the Congressional Gold Medal, the highest honor the United States bestows on a civilian.

    • ~Other recipients include: George Washington, Thomas Edison, Betty Ford and Mother Teresa.

  • October 24, 2005 - Rosa Parks died at the age of 92, having become the first woman in the nation's history to lie in state at the U.S. Capitol.
